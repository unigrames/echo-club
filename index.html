Welcome to Eco Club

Discover the beauty and benefits of plants.

The Eco Club at MIMS is a vibrant community dedicated to promoting environmental awareness and sustainability. Our mission is to inspire and educate individuals about the importance of preserving nature and adopting eco-friendly practices. Through various initiatives, workshops, and programs, we aim to create a greener and healthier planet. The club organizes tree plantation drives, waste management campaigns, and educational sessions on topics like renewable energy, biodiversity, and conservation. We also focus on showcasing the benefits of plants, their medicinal uses, and their role in maintaining ecological balance. Join us in our journey to make a positive impact on the environment and foster a culture of sustainability.

Plant Information

Aloe Vera

Scientific Name: Aloe vera

Family: Liliaceae

Parts Used: Leaves

Propagation: Suckers

Medicinal Uses

  • Being a good pithanashaka, acts as a diuretic, analgesic, and relieves constipation.
  • It helps to stabilize blood sugar levels, hence is good for diabetes.
  • It gives relief from wounds and ulcers when juice is applied with jeera powder. It also helps to treat fungus, insect bites, herpes, vaginal infections, and allergic reactions.
  • Pulp is known for many cosmetic uses; almost all shampoos and soaps contain aloe as an ingredient. It prevents the growth of acne and moisturizes the skin.
  • Pulp is a big drug for menstrual problems.
  • Juice with turmeric is a good tonic for liver and spleen enlargement and also for amenorrhea and piles.
  • Aloe juice with a pinch of pippali and honey is a good remedy for fever.
  • It is antiseptic and helps to kill many viruses and bacteria.
  • It helps to alkalize the body, balances acid production, and treats constipation.
  • It prevents kidney stones, Crohn's disease, and IBS.
  • It also controls the levels of cholesterol and oxidative stress.
